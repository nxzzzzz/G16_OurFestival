The origin of Halloween

Halloween's meaning stems from the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, a harvest celebration marking the boundary between worlds where spirits could return, blended with the Christian observance of All Saints' Day (All Hallows' Day) on November 1st; the word "Halloween" itself is a shortening of "All Hallows' Eve," the evening before this holy day.
